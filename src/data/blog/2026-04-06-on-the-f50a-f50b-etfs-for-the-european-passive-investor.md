I often feel grateful for living in a world where I do not have to make asset allocation my entire personality. All you need to do is follow a handful of basic principles, and you'll soon find yourself outperforming many active investors.

If you're anything like me, you'll still spend way too much time on those remaining handful of principles.

So what do we focus on first? Picking the right asset to invest in of course. At this point pretty much every personal finance reddit has settled on the same conclusion. You place your funds in a globally diversified index-tracking ETF such as the IWDA/EMIM pair and call it a day. 

[*Indexes were never designed to be investments.*](https://youtu.be/mqIHa6URUPk) In reality our ETFs underperform perfection by an estimated 0.3%-0.8%. Adverse selection, price impact, and mean reversion all cause our beloved ETFs to bleed value every year. The dividends the ETF collects are often taxed at the source, causing further underperformance.


I don't want to underperform perfection.
